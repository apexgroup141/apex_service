Cooling planning

AC, mini-split and heat pump cooling options compared clearly.

Cooling problems can come from the outdoor unit, indoor coil, refrigerant circuit, electrical parts, thermostat settings, blocked filters or ductwork that cannot move enough air. A good cooling estimate should not jump straight to equipment replacement without checking the basics.

For homes with older central AC, the conversation often includes whether to repair the existing unit, replace it with a new AC system, add targeted ductless comfort or move to a heat pump that handles both heating and cooling. The goal is a practical recommendation that fits the home, not a one-size-fits-all system.

  • Central AC diagnostics, repair and replacement
  • Mini-split installation for rooms, additions, garages and ADUs
  • Heat pump cooling upgrades for year-round comfort
  • Frozen coil, warm air and short cycling troubleshooting
  • Airflow, thermostat and electrical checks before replacement

When to call

Call when the system runs but does not cool, rooms stay humid, breakers trip, coils freeze, airflow drops, the outdoor unit is loud or utility bills climb without a clear reason.

Clean installation details

Cooling work should include equipment placement, drain routing, line-set planning, electrical review, startup testing and a walkthrough of controls before the job is considered finished.

Cooling FAQ

Useful answers before approving cooling work.

Should I repair or replace my AC?

It depends on age, repair cost, refrigerant type, comfort performance, electrical condition and whether a heat pump would be a better long-term upgrade.

Can ductless solve one hot room?

Often, yes. A single-zone mini-split can be a strong option for a bedroom, office, addition, garage or room that existing ducts do not serve well.

Why does AC freeze up?

Frozen coils can point to airflow restrictions, dirty filters, coil issues, refrigerant problems or blower trouble. The cause should be diagnosed before the system is restarted repeatedly.
